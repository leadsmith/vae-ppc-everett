Common Questions

Answers to What Families Ask Us Most

If you're still on the fence, these answers address the most common hesitations we hear from families just like yours.

How much does in-home care cost in Everett, WA?

Costs vary based on the level of care and hours needed. Many families are surprised to find that in-home care is significantly more affordable than assisted living facilities. We provide a full breakdown during your free consultation — no hidden fees, no surprises. Some long-term care insurance policies and VA benefits also apply.

How quickly can care actually start?

In most cases, care can begin within 48–72 hours of your initial consultation. If your situation is urgent, tell us — we have a fast-start process for families who need immediate help. We don't make you wait weeks to get support.

What if my parent refuses help or doesn't want a caregiver?

This is the most common concern we hear. Our care coordinators are experienced in this exact situation. We'll discuss strategies during your consultation and often recommend starting with just a few hours per week in a very low-pressure way. Many seniors who initially resisted now look forward to their caregiver's visits.

Can I trust a caregiver in my parent's home?

Every caregiver goes through extensive criminal background checks, reference verification, and skills assessment before they're placed. You also meet and approve your caregiver before care begins. And you can request a replacement caregiver at any time — no explanation needed.

Do you provide dementia or Alzheimer's care?

Yes. We have caregivers specifically trained in dementia and memory care. This includes behavioral approaches, routine-building, safe redirection, and compassionate communication. We also offer our SELECT program to help match seniors with cognitive conditions to the most compatible caregiver.

Is there a long-term commitment or contract required?

No. Care is provided on a flexible, month-to-month basis. You can increase, decrease, or stop services as your family's needs change. There are no cancellation penalties. This flexibility is something families consistently tell us they value most.
